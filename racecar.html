3 Wheel Electric Racecar

October 2013 - June 2014

Project Overview

This car was originally built during the 2012-2013 school year by 15 students in Foothill High School's advanced engineering course. Everything, including the welded steel-tubing frame and all the electronics, were built and assembled by students. In UCI's 2013 Energy Invitational race this car placed 2nd overall.

As president of the school's Engineering Club, I led a team to improve the car for the 2014 race. We worked for several months to address 5 main issues. Improvements included inserting a gear reduction box to change the steering ratio, fabricating boxes to make batteries interchangeable, remounting shocks to lower the front end, adjusting wheel toe and camber, and adding sheet metal to the cockpit to protect the driver. The car finished much improved, passed all pre-race safety inspections, and we won the 2014 competition!

Steering Ratio

A high steering ratio on the original car meant the driver had to awkwardly shuffle the steering wheel during tight corners, costing precious seconds. To remedy this issue, we replaced a portion of the steering column with a gear reduction box. In the proper orientation, this amplifies steering input allowing the driver to reach both extreme steering positions with ~270Â° of steering input and without changing hand position.

Battery Boxes

The biggest hinderance to the car's success in 2013 was power. The car is powered with 6 deep-cycle batteries, which can discharge a large capacity--good for racing--but take long to recharge. The competition allows one hour of recharge time between each of four 15 minute heats--not enough to fully recharge the batteries. By the fourth heat in 2013, the car struggled to accelerate past walking speed. The club's budget allowed one major expenditure, and we elected to purchase a second set of batteries. This would allow over closer to two hours of charge time for each set of batteries.

To make this strategy viable, removable battery trays were constructed to allow quick battery pack changes. Shown below, they replaced the semi-permanent mounting system previously used. The trays slide out horizontally toward the back of the car, are held in place by 4 bolts and both can be switched in about five minutes. Four trays were fabricated in total. I designed the pack and welded all the components together.
