Magnetic Levitation Control

March 2016

Project Overview

In this lab for ME C134 (Feedback Control Systems), my team and I designed a controller for a provided electromagnet levitation setup and implemented it using analog circuitry. To achieve this, the transfer function of the analog controller was derived from circuit diagrams and component values for unassigned resistors were computed from experimentally determined linearized system gains. After building the circuit, resistance values were tuned using potentiometers to achieve the desired levitation response.

Check out the video of the final product above! Besides the original steel marble, we also managed to tune the system to levitate a steel ring and an adjustment knob from a desk chair.
