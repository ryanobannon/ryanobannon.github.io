Slider Crank

Summer 2015

Project Overview

I designed and manufactured this system as a test-bed platform for an Autodesk and National Instruments-funded research project in Summer 2015. Three other undergraduate researchers and I used this slider crank to test the limitations of software bridging the simulation gap between Autodesk's Inventor CAD package and National Instruments' LabVIEW environment. Different rigid, rotating, sliding and flexible spring joints enabled us to gauge the abilities and failings of several iterations of the new software based on the open-source Modelica language.

The joint collaboration between Autodesk, National Instruments and UC Berkeley to develop this new software was announced at National Instruments' NIWeek in August 2015. See media coverage here.
